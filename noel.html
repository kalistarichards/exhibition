Essay:

My essay explains, develops, and circles around a big idea. My big idea was about Philippines still being a place with thankful people even though it's a third world country. My essay weaves in, seamlessy, the voices of others. These voices could include quotes from reading, sources on the internet, song lyrics, overheard conversations, or family sayings to push against, agree with, or flesh out our thinking. How I came up with the idea of writing an essay about the Philippines is because of my father. He had talked about it in our interview for World History, which was conducting research on the personal history of a family member.

Child Labor Poem:

When I was just a little boy I’ve worked at the N.Y. Button Works. Working in the harshest conditions Covered in dirt as black as coal. When I was just a little boy Instead of being a normal child who’d play outside My mother forced me into this factory Like a moth to the flame that I am. When I was just a little boy Working on the 1st floor, Twining and twining Getting my little fingers caught in the machines That often ran so quickly, Quicker than the speed of light. When I was just a little boy I dreaded going to work The factory was a prison. As a little boy I just wanted to go to school Have the time of my life But I couldn’t.

Social Media + Relationships Poem:

There are too many things, Too many things I want to do with you. Baseball games In the blistering heat So I can Instagram our love. Trips to the city Tweeting, Vaguely enough to make people wonder, How much fun we had that night. I tried my best My very best to impress, Impress you and only you on instagram I even went ham. As I liked your photos To get your attention It felt like a hike. I was hoping you’d comment on my photos But I bet you’d rather vomit that night. Even though I haven’t been serious in the past I wanted to make sure we’d last. I’m searching and searching For info about you And what I should do Since I’m interested in you. Even though I’m as nervous as a long tailed cat In a room full of rocking chairs, I still tried to flirt and talk to you in person But it just seems to stench of failure. Even though I haven’t been serious in the past I’m going to make sure we’d last.
